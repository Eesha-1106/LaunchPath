Next Step

Key Points

  • Check demand – use tools like Google Trends and keyword planners to see if interest is rising
  • Do competitor analysis (SWOT): what are rivals’ strengths and where do they fall short
  • Identify your target user persona.

Challenges

  • You may find too much data and struggle to filter noise.
  • Early markets can have sparse data (niche ideas). Be wary of bias in surveys.
  • Competitors with entrenched users can block entry.

Tools & Templates

  • Google Trends (free market signals), SimilarWeb or Crunchbase (competitor traffic/funding)
  • Typeform/SurveyMonkey for surveys, AnswerThePublic for customer questions
  • Templates like a Competitor Analysis chart or Customer Persona canvas help organize research (Asana offers a free competitor template)

Examples & Tips

Example

A startup developing electric scooters might check Google Trends for “electric scooter demand” and survey local commuters. If major players overlook serving college campuses, that niche could be an opportunity.

Tips

  • Confirm there’s a real demand – if few search or talk about the problem, it may not be big enough
  • Identify niche gaps competitors ignore
  • Look at adjacent markets for inspiration (think laterally).
