Key Points

  • : Launch a test version (e.g. an email sign-up page, simple prototype) and drive some traffic to it.
  • DMeasure engagement to decide if to persevere or pivot. Define your business model: decide how you’ll make money (subscription, ads, affiliate, transaction fees, etc.)
  • Early on, choose one revenue stream to simplify focus.

Challenges

  • Small-scale tests can give false positives/negatives.
  • You might misinterpret casual interest as demand.
  • Choosing the right revenue model is tricky – some models (ads) need scale, others (subscription) need strong product-market fit.

Tools & Templates

  • Carrd, Unbounce or Landingi for quick landing pages. Stripe or PayPal to prototype payments.
  • Typeform or Google Forms to capture leads. Use a Business Model Canvas (Strategyzer) or Lean Canvas (Ash Maurya) template to clarify product, customers, channels and revenue.

Examples & Tips

Example

Buffer famously launched with just a “Plan your Tweets” landing page describing the product and a signup form. By tracking signups they iterated the idea. (This lean approach is in line with Ries’s MVP concept)

Tips

  • Run a smoke test: advertise the MVP to see how many sign up. Pre-sales or demos are great – e.g. offer a discount for early signups.
  • Be ready to pivot if validation fails. Choose a revenue model aligned with your customers’ habits (e.g. consumers prefer subscriptions or freemium; enterprises may pay per seat).
