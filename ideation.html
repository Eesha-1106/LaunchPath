Key Points

  • Spot everyday issues (ask “what frustrates me?”)
  • brainstorm solutions
  • Look at personal experiences or niche markets for pains that others might overlook.

Challenges

  • It’s easy to fall into analysis paralysis (overthinking possible ideas) or to chase the “shiny new thing” without a real problem
  • Avoid copying obvious ideas; instead stay user-focused.
  • Many founders struggle with too many ideas or with bias toward their own ideas without feedback.

Tools & Templates

  • Use whiteboard/mind-map tools (e.g. Miro, MindMeister) for ideation
  • Notion or Trello can capture and organize ideas. (Templates like a Lean Canvas or an Idea Evaluation checklist can help structure your thinking.)

Examples & Tips

Example

A classic example is Slack – it began when its founders needed better team communication and realized others did too. By solving their own “real problem,” they created a global product.

Tips

  • Look for minor annoyances (if something bugs you, it may bug others).
  • Think of improving existing solutions
  • Validate early with peers: talk about ideas before investing in them
  • Prioritize ideas that feel tech-backed or scalable.
